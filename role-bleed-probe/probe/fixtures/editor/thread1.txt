Most LLM evals quietly measure the wrong thing.
They score the final answer and call it rigor. But a model can reach the right
answer through reasoning that is completely confabulated. It's worth noting that
when the chain-of-thought disagrees with the action, your monitor is logging
fiction. I learned this the hard way watching a grader pass answers it never
actually checked. The fix isn't a better prompt. It's measuring whether the
trace is FAITHFUL to what the agent did, not just whether the output is correct.
